CSS-101: Selectors & Specificity

Understanding how the browser resolves conflicting style rules is the foundation of writing maintainable CSS. Every rule you write participates in a weighted scoring system — and knowing how those scores add up will save you from the endless spiral of !important declarations.

The Cascade in Brief

When multiple rules target the same element, the browser picks the winner based on three factors: origin and importance, specificity, and source order. Specificity is the one that trips people up most often, so let's break it down.

Calculating Specificity

Think of specificity as a four-column score: inline styles, IDs, classes/pseudo-classes/attributes, and elements/pseudo-elements. A selector like #header .nav a:hover scores 0-1-2-1 — one ID, two classes, one element. The highest score in the leftmost column wins.

Keep It Flat

A good rule of thumb: if your selector is more than three levels deep, it's time to refactor. Prefer a single class name over deeply nested selectors. Your future self (and anyone inheriting your code) will thank you.
